Business Model Canvas — AS IS
Key Partners

Charitable foundations — provide funding and medical supplies. Irregular and one-off contributions.

University & academic department — partnership exists formally but functions primarily as an oversight body rather than a collaborative one.

Gap
Media partnerships — absent; identified as a strategic need.

Competitors: Regional Hospital on Orly St., Dnister Hospital.

Key Activities

Outpatient consultations and diagnostics

Ambulatory (day) treatment

Inpatient treatment

Value Proposition

Strength
Comprehensive multi-specialty care — no patient is turned away.

High clinical expertise, including complex and rare cases.

Child-centred ethos: staff treat patients as part of an extended family.

Informal only
Empathy and care are delivered at an individual level but not embedded in organisational processes or service standards.

Key Resources

People — specialist physicians, junior medical staff, administrative and support staff.

Physical infrastructure — medical equipment, facilities, vehicles.

Constraint
Budget — fully controlled by external authorities. No internal financial autonomy. Communication with the finance function is fragmented.

Channels

Facebook page, website — present but underutilised.

Regional outreach visits — legally mandated mobile consultation brigades sent to rural areas.

Ineffective
Outreach visits function as social events rather than clinical or promotional activity.

TV and radio — used occasionally; staff participation is low due to reluctance to appear publicly.

Customer Relationships

Critical gap
Relationships are strained. Legacy institutional culture produces dismissive and at times hostile communication from administrative and junior staff.

No structured service standards or patient communication protocols in place.

Customer Segments

Current segment
Ukrainian families with children aged 0–18, middle-income.

Target segment
Families with above-average income — willing to pay for higher service standards.

Gap
No active patient acquisition strategy. Retention efforts are minimal and informal.

Cost Structure

Facilities & utilities — covered by the Regional Government Authority.

Staff salaries — funded from the state budget at a fixed rate. Under healthcare reform, shifting to activity-based reimbursement tied to services delivered (NHIF contracts).

Revenue Streams

Single source
State budget funding — primary income; not tied to performance or patient volume.

Charitable donations & foundation grants — supplementary and unpredictable.

Missing
No paid services. No insurance partnerships. No commercial revenue of any kind.
