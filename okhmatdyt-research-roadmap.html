Research Roadmap
⚡ Project reframe
Initial brief: "Help doctors be more welcoming." Reframed after kick-off: the problem was structural, not behavioural. The entire service model needed redesigning for a competitive, patient-centric market.
1
Understanding the system — Business Model Workshops
3 workshops · Stakeholder alignment
What & How
3 co-creation workshops with hospital leadership + NHIF
Business Model Canvas: AS IS → TO BE
Stakeholder alignment on constraints and opportunities
Why this method
Before designing, needed shared understanding of the whole system
Multiple stakeholders with conflicting priorities — needed alignment first
Canvas forced explicit conversation about what was fixed vs changeable
Output & Key insight
BMC AS IS BMC TO BE Revenue stream map
Okhmatdyt cannot be a commercial business — only a social business. Revenue is a means to better care, not a goal.
↓
2
Market exploration — Service Safari
Competitive research · Private clinics
What & How
Visited private children's clinics as a regular customer
Observed: services, pricing, payment flow, patient experience
Benchmarked against Sinevo lab offering
Why this method
Before asking patients what they'd pay for — needed to see what already worked in the market
Surveys would give aspirations; safari gave reality
Legislation said what was legal; safari showed what was viable
Output & Key insight
Paid services shortlist Competitive synthesis
Clear shortlist: after-hours consultations, lab tests, equipment diagnostics bundled with specialist consultation.
↓
3
Measuring current quality — eNPS Survey
Quantitative baseline · n=42
What & How
Structured satisfaction survey at point of care
Measured: overall satisfaction, touchpoints, NPS
Dual NPS: separate score for hospital AND for individual doctor
Why this method
Needed quantitative baseline before any design work
Dual NPS deliberately separated medical quality from service quality
Gave data to justify recommendations to leadership
Output & Key insight
eNPS baseline report Satisfaction analysis
Overall NPS: +21 · CSI: 63% · Doctor NPS: +61 · Service NPS: −14. Gap of 75 points. Medical quality ≠ service quality — completely different interventions needed.
↓
4
Understanding the patient journey — Semi-structured Interviews
Qualitative · n=30 · Self-referrals only
What & How
30 in-depth interviews with self-referring parents
Focused on: decision to come, experience, comparison with private clinics
Deliberate scoping: self-referrals only (not emergency, not GP-referred)
Why this method
Self-referrals = only segment that actively chooses Okhmatdyt
Only segment where hospital can compete, differentiate and charge
Survey gave numbers; interviews gave the why behind them
Output & Key insight
Persona (Segment 2) 1× Customer Journey Map (outpatient self-referral) Value Proposition Canvas
Reception is the most damaging touchpoint — encountered 3 times. Booking gives zero advantage. Informal payments create unfair queues.
↓
5
Going deeper — Contextual Interviews + Observation
n=10 doctors · 1 week field observation
What & How
10 contextual interviews with doctors across departments
1 week on-site: 1 full day per department
Watched: how records completed, how Dr. Eleks used (or not)
Why this method
Digitisation introduced but adoption inconsistent
Needed to understand actual workflow, not intended one
Couldn't design training without seeing real friction points first
Output & Key insight
Workflow analysis Training programme design
Patient data entered manually 3+ times across separate paper systems. No automatic data flow. Training had to target specific friction, not generic onboarding.
↓
6
Synthesis — Service Blueprint + Recommendations
All streams combined · Prioritised output
What & How
All research streams synthesised into service blueprint
AS IS mapped: customer, frontstage, backstage, support systems
TO BE interventions mapped per stage
Recommendations prioritised by impact vs effort
Why this method
Blueprint made the invisible visible — staff saw the gap they were creating
Connects patient experience to backend operations in one view
Gives leadership an actionable, prioritised plan — not just a list of problems
Output & Outcomes
Service Blueprint AS IS + TO BE Recommendations + priorities
✓ Paid services launched  ·  ✓ Dr. Eleks operational  ·  ✓ Bonus system designed
