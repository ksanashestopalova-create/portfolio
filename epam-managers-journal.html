How the knowledge base was used

Entries were accumulated weekly across the full intervention period. The HR Business Partner maintained two versions: a full version (with manager attribution, for HR BP and unit head only) and a shared version (anonymised, distributed to all managers in the unit).

The complexity classification served a second purpose: cases rated M0–M1 were passed to the Manager School as ready-made teaching material — real situations the unit had actually faced, with documented solutions. This connected the knowledge base directly to the management development pipeline.

The journal was designed to be self-sustaining: managers began consulting it independently before bringing new situations to the community, which over time reduced the volume of escalations and built individual judgment capacity.

Example entry (anonymised)
Sample case · illustrative only
Submitted by
Manager A · session 4
Case description
Engineer with 4 years tenure, consistently strong delivery, failed assessment for the second time. No clear feedback from assessment committee on what to improve. Engineer is disengaged and has stopped asking about next steps.
What the manager did
Scheduled a 1:1, tried to reassure. Engineer remained withdrawn. Manager unsure whether to push for re-assessment or wait.
Resolution status
Unresolved at submission
Community recommendation
Request written feedback from assessment committee — verbal "didn't pass" is not actionable. Build a concrete 3-month development plan with the engineer, co-authored. Schedule bi-weekly check-ins specifically on assessment progress, not general 1:1s. Re-assessment should be the engineer's decision once they feel ready.
Who should be involved
HR BP · manager · assessment committee contact
Complexity level
M0 M1 — Junior manager M2 M3
