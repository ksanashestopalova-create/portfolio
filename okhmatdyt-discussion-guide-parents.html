Parent / Patient Interviews
Participants
Parents of children, self-referrals only
Duration
45–60 minutes
Format
Semi-structured, in-person or remote
Interviewer script — opening
Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. My name is Oksana, and I'm a researcher working with Okhmatdyt Children's Hospital. We're trying to understand the experience of families who visit the hospital — what works well, what's difficult, and how we can improve. There are no right or wrong answers. I'm interested in your honest experience, including anything that frustrated you. The conversation will take about 45–60 minutes. With your permission, I'd like to take notes. Everything you share is confidential and will only be used in aggregate form to inform improvements. Do you have any questions before we begin?
1
Warm-up — Getting to know the participant
~5 min
Goal: build rapport, understand context, no pressure
Can you tell me a little about your family — how old is your child, and how long have you been coming to Okhmatdyt?
  • probeIs this your first visit or have you been here before?
  • probeDo you live in Lviv or did you travel to come here?
2
Decision to come — Awareness & choice
~10 min
Goal: understand what triggered the visit and why Okhmatdyt specifically
Can you walk me through what happened that led you to come to Okhmatdyt this time? What was going on with your child?
  • probeWas this an urgent situation or something you planned in advance?
  • probeDid your family doctor recommend you come here, or did you decide yourself?
Why did you choose Okhmatdyt specifically? What made you decide to come here rather than somewhere else?
  • probeHad someone recommended it to you?
  • probeDid you look at other options — private clinics, other hospitals?
  • probeWhat did you know about Okhmatdyt before you came?
How did you find the information you needed — the address, the phone number, which doctor to see?
  • probeDid you look at the website? Was it helpful?
  • probeDid you ask friends or family? Online groups?
  • probeWas it easy or difficult to find out what you needed?
3
Booking & arrival — First contact with the hospital
~10 min
Goal: understand the booking process and first impressions — key friction area
Can you walk me through how you booked your appointment? What did you do, step by step?
  • probeDid you call the reception? What was that like?
  • probeWere you able to get an appointment at a time that worked for you?
  • probeDid anything surprise or frustrate you during that process?
⚠ Listen for: tone of the receptionist, clarity of information, whether they felt the booking was confirmed or uncertain.
When you arrived at the hospital, what happened first? Can you describe that experience?
  • probeWas it easy to find the reception or the right department?
  • probeDid you need to queue or wait at reception? How long?
  • probeHow did the staff at reception interact with you?
  • probeWere you asked to provide information you'd already given when booking?
⚠ Key area: double data entry, rude reception, token/queue confusion. Let them tell it — don't lead.
4
Waiting — The hardest part
~8 min
Goal: understand waiting experience, fairness perception, environment
After you checked in, how long did you wait before seeing the doctor? Can you describe what that wait was like?
  • probeWhere did you wait — was there a designated area?
  • probeHow was your child during the wait? Was there anything to keep them occupied?
  • probeDid the wait feel fair — did you understand why it was taking long?
Did you notice other people being seen before you even though they arrived later? What did you think about that?
  • probeDid you understand why that happened?
  • probeHow did that make you feel?
⚠ This surfaces the informal payment / queue-jumping issue without leading the participant directly to it.
5
The appointment — Doctor experience
~10 min
Goal: understand what worked well — doctor quality is the key strength
Can you tell me about your experience with the doctor? What was the appointment like?
  • probeDid the doctor explain the diagnosis clearly? Did you understand what was wrong?
  • probeHow did the doctor interact with your child?
  • probeDid you feel listened to and taken seriously?
  • probeWere you given clear instructions about treatment and next steps?
Was there anything about the appointment itself that was confusing or unclear?
  • probeThe medical card — did anything happen with it that you found difficult?
  • probeWere prescriptions or referrals easy to understand?
  • probeDid anyone ask you to pay, or was it unclear whether payment was expected?
⚠ Payment ambiguity is a key insight. Approach gently — people may feel embarrassed about the informal payment system.
6
After the visit — Follow-up and overall impression
~8 min
Goal: understand what happens after, satisfaction, loyalty, comparison with private
Did you need to come back for a follow-up? How did you arrange that?
  • probeWas it easy to book the follow-up, or did you run into the same difficulties as before?
  • probeWere you given a specific time, or was it uncertain?
Have you also used private children's clinics? If so, how would you compare the experience?
  • probeWhat does a private clinic do better, in your experience?
  • probeIs there anything Okhmatdyt offers that private clinics don't or can't?
  • probeIf Okhmatdyt offered paid services with clear pricing, would you consider using them? What would make that feel worthwhile?
Would you come back to Okhmatdyt? Would you recommend it to other parents?
  • probeWhat would you say to a friend who was deciding whether to come here?
  • probeIs there one thing that, if it changed, would make the experience significantly better?
Closing script
Thank you so much — this has been really valuable. Before we finish, is there anything else about your experience that you'd like to share that we haven't covered? Anything that stood out — positively or negatively? [pause] We really appreciate your honesty. Your feedback will directly inform how we improve things for families coming here in the future.
