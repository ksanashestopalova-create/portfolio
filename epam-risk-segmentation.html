People Risk Segmentation Framework

HR Business Partner · EPAM Systems · Reconstructed from field research, 2018
Input signals (sourced from Telescope analytics platform)
📅
Tenure
Time in company. Longer tenure = potentially higher impact of structural disruption.
🔴
Risk status
Red / Yellow / Green flag set by manager or HR at last 1:1. Includes date of last update.
👤
Manager
Which manager owns this person. Flags weaker managers for closer monitoring.
📊
Last assessment
Did the person pass their last level assessment? Was a salary review triggered?
⚠️
Project risk
Risk flag set by delivery manager. Cross-referenced with people risk.
Core design principle

Risk status is not static. A green flag older than 30 days is treated as yellow — because without recent contact, we have no valid signal. A yellow flag with no recent conversation is treated as red. Recency of information is part of the risk calculation.

Priority tiers
Priority Conditions Rationale Action
P1 · Critical
Red risk updated within last 30 days
+ assessment not passed or salary review pending
 Active dissatisfaction signal combined with unresolved career blocker. Highest flight risk. Interview within 48 hours. Flag to unit head.
P2 · High
Red risk — assessment passed, salary reviewed
 Risk flag present but career is progressing. May be workload, culture, or manager quality. Needs diagnosis. Interview within 1 week.
P3 · Elevated
Yellow risk — any status
OR Green risk — last contact >30 days ago
 Green without recent contact = unverified green. Treated as yellow until confirmed. Stale data is a risk in itself. Interview within 2 weeks.
P4 · Monitor
Yellow risk — conversation within last 30 days
 Known concern, recently discussed. Situation is tracked. Lower urgency but still needs follow-through. Follow up in current interview cycle.
P5 · Routine
Green risk — conversation within last 30 days
 Fresh signal, no flags. Still interviewed — baseline is needed and everyone deserves contact. Include in scheduled interview cycle.
Secondary sort: by manager

Within each priority tier, interviews were additionally sorted by manager — grouping each manager's people together. This ensured that by end of each week, every manager received structured feedback about their own cohort, enabling targeted follow-up conversations between the HR Business Partner and each manager individually.
